Tell me about yourself

I gravitate towards problems with numerical foundations. At Outfittery, I grew conversion by 120% by scaling experimentation culture and tackling the 60% post-acquisition drop-off. FORMEL SKIN is a deliberate choice: it combines subscription mechanics with a Trust Dimension that makes CRO more meaningful.

Category 1: CRO & Experimentation Depth
1
Experimentation framework from scratch — the hardest decisions?

Tooling is secondary to Governance. The hardest decision was enforcing a cap on concurrent tests to prevent data contamination. I used a 5-layer stack: Multi-Signal (Tableau/Hotjar), Hypothesis (ICE), Protocol, Governance, and Knowledge Sharing.

Category 2: Product Strategy & Thinking
9
Your first 30-60-90 days?

30 Days: Audit the "Data-Trust Gap" (Funnel drop-off vs CS logs).
60 Days: Ship high-signal experiment on plan selection.
90 Days: Build the "Learning Library" for cross-vertical scale (Weight Loss, Menopause).

10
Diagnose conversion opportunities without data?

I perform a Heuristic Audit across three personas: The Sceptic, The Ailing, and The Optimiser. I look for where medical jargon becomes a friction point rather than an authority builder.

11
Subscription model optimisation — what levers matter most?

The biggest lever is surviving the Break-in Phase. Focus on Week-2 activation. Secondary levers: Dunning management and "Interval Flexibility" (pause vs cancel).

Category 3: AI & Modern Development
17
How would you use AI to accelerate CRO at VOY specifically?

Drop-off Prediction: Use session behaviour (hesitation on photo upload) to trigger real-time trust interventions. LLM Questionnaire: Dynamically rewrite jargon into conversational language based on user profile.

18
AI-generated personalisation — opportunities and risks?

Opportunity: Personalising the "break-in" phase narrative. Risk: AI generating unverified medical advice. Mitigation: Clinician-approved content matrix for all AI outputs.

Category 4: Leadership & Team Building
25
Managing a designer who disagrees with your data?

Shift from Subjective Opinion to Variable Testing. Run a small pilot. If the data supports the designer, we scale. This maintains psychological safety while upholding analytical rigour.

26
Managing up: Leadership moving too fast for the data?

Quantify the Risk of Speed. "We can launch today at 75% confidence, but the 25% risk of a false positive could cost us €X in LTV. Are we accepting that financial risk?"

Category 5: Health Tech & VOY Specific
31
Optimising questionnaires without clinical compromise?

Positive Friction. I don't remove steps; I improve the "Perceived Value." If a user understands why a photo is needed for their bespoke formula, completion rates increase.

32
Consent and data sensitivity in the funnel?

Just-in-Time Consent. Don't ask for a blanket medical agreement at Step 1. Ask for photo permissions exactly when the camera is triggered, stating: "Only your assigned doctor sees this."

Category 6: Hard Challenge / Pressure Questions
37
What is a strongly held belief you have that most disagree with?
"Optimising for Conversion Rate is often the wrong goal. I value Quality-Adjusted Conversion. I would rather drop CVR by 20% to halve my cancellation rate. LTV is the only true signal."
38
If our conversion rate is already best-in-class, what do you work on?

Activation Quality. High CVR means nothing if Day-30 churn is high. I'd move to post-acquisition "First 30-Day Outcomes" and subscription upsell surfaces inside the patient dashboard.
